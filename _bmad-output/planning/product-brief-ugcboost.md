# Product Brief: UGCBoost

## Executive Summary

UGCBoost is a curated marketplace connecting brands with vetted UGC creators in Kazakhstan. Unlike existing platforms that function as open bulletin boards flooded with low-quality applicants, UGCBoost enforces a two-stage moderation process — automated metrics screening followed by manual quality review — ensuring brands only interact with creators who meet professional standards.

The platform solves a critical scaling problem: brands want to run mass UGC seeding campaigns (50–500 creators per campaign), but the operational overhead of sourcing, vetting, contracting, and tracking creators manually makes this unsustainable. UGCBoost absorbs this complexity — from creator curation and contract management (via TrustMe e-signing) to campaign analytics (via LiveDune) — letting brands focus on creative strategy rather than operations.

No dedicated UGC marketplace exists in Kazakhstan today. International platforms (Billo, Insense, Trend.io) ignore the CIS market entirely. With Instagram reaching 10M users and TikTok 7–8M in Kazakhstan, combined with booming e-commerce demand for authentic content, UGCBoost enters a market with clear demand and zero localized competition.

Early validation confirms this: a basic landing page at ugcboost.kz — with no marketing or promotion — organically attracted inbound inquiries from One Corporate (travel holding), Beauty by Sofia (major cosmetics distributor), and First Media Group (marketing holding representing Kotex, Ferrero, Milka). Brands are actively searching for UGC solutions and finding none.

## The Problem

Brands increasingly rely on UGC seeding — distributing products to dozens or hundreds of micro-creators who post native content on their own pages. This delivers higher engagement than traditional influencer deals, at a fraction of the cost, with guaranteed posts (not just stories).

But the process is broken:

- **Discovery is noise.** Every second person calls themselves a UGC creator. When a brand opens applications via Google Forms or Instagram comments, they receive hundreds of irrelevant submissions — wrong category, poor content quality, insufficient reach.
- **Vetting is manual labor.** For each applicant, someone must check content quality, visual aesthetic, category fit, posting frequency, and average views. This means hiring a dedicated employee just for creator management.
- **Operations don't scale.** Collecting addresses for product shipping, managing contracts, tracking deliverables, chasing missing posts — the operational load grows linearly with each creator added.
- **Legal compliance is a barrier.** Under Kazakhstan tax law, companies on the standard taxation regime must work with registered businesses (ТОО/ИП) to claim marketing expenses as deductions. Most UGC creators are individuals with no tax registration, making paid collaborations legally problematic for brands.

Existing apps in Kazakhstan (TusApp, TTfluence, and others) replicate the same bulletin-board model online — no moderation, no quality filtering, no operational value-add. For brands, there is no reason to switch from Instagram DMs to an app that offers the same unfiltered experience.

## The Solution

UGCBoost is a three-sided platform: **creators**, **brands**, and **admin (operator)**.

**For UGC Creators:**
- Apply via a web landing page with profile details, social media links, content category, city, and physical address (for product deliveries)
- Identity verification — confirm ownership of linked social media accounts to prevent impersonation and scam
- Pass two-stage moderation: automated screening (follower count, average views, engagement rate, posting frequency via LiveDune) followed by manual quality review with personalized feedback on rejections
- Sign a service agreement electronically via TrustMe — one-time signing grants platform access; contract includes content licensing rights (creators agree that brands may repurpose their content for paid ads, at UGCBoost's discretion)
- Browse and apply to campaigns matching their approved category; review full brief before applying with confirmation step to prevent accidental submissions
- Submit completed work by attaching links to published content (Instagram, TikTok, and Threads) with configurable minimum fields per campaign requirements, optional extra links for bonus content, and automatic duplicate detection

**For Brands:**
- Verified company accounts (preventing impersonation and reputational risk)
- Create campaigns in three categories: **product** (physical or digital), **service**, or **event** — each with tailored brief fields
- Define detailed briefs: content type (Reels, TikTok, Threads), requirements, tagging instructions, barter/paid terms, creator category, and capacity limits
- Review and approve/reject pre-vetted creator applications for each campaign
- **Campaign content gallery:** visual tile-based dashboard showing all published UGC content across a campaign — video thumbnails (auto-playing muted or paused), with per-post metrics (views, likes, comments) displayed in a format familiar from social networks. Designed as a "wow moment" for brand managers: open a campaign and instantly see the full wall of content your creators produced
- **Creator rating and feedback:** brands rate each creator's work (e.g., 1–10 scale) and leave qualitative feedback. Ratings are internal to the platform — not shown to creators — and help UGCBoost track quality trends, identify top performers, and build data for future creator ranking. Negative feedback is handled privately to avoid discouraging creators over subjective brand expectations
- Access aggregated campaign analytics — views, engagement, comments — automatically pulled and updated via LiveDune
- Content licensing: on premium tiers, brands can repurpose UGC content as paid ad creatives (Spark Ads, Partnership Ads); UGCBoost controls licensing rights as an intermediary
- Legal compliance: UGCBoost acts as an intermediary agency on the standard tax regime (VAT payer), enabling brands to claim UGC marketing expenses as tax deductions

**For the Operator (Admin):**
- Moderation dashboard for creator applications with approval/rejection workflow and feedback
- Campaign creation and management
- Creator assignment tracking across campaigns (visibility into which creators are already committed)
- Contract management and compliance oversight

## What Makes This Different

**Curated, not open.** The core differentiator is quality control. Every creator on UGCBoost has passed automated metrics screening and manual review by an industry expert. Brands skip the vetting grind entirely.

**Truly native content through unlabeled creators.** Today, most UGC creators write "UGC creator" in their Instagram/TikTok/Threads bio so brands can find them. This backfires: when audiences see the label, they immediately recognize the content as paid promotion, reducing its effectiveness. UGCBoost eliminates this problem. Since creators no longer need to advertise themselves publicly — the platform matches them with brands — they can remove the "UGC creator" label from their profiles entirely. UGCBoost recommends this and provides a clear business incentive: brands prefer creators whose content looks organic, so unlabeled creators get selected for more campaigns. The result is content that audiences trust more, which is exactly what brands are paying for.

**Operational infrastructure, not just a listing.** UGCBoost handles contracting (TrustMe), analytics (LiveDune), and will add logistics (Relog integration for optimized delivery routes). It replaces the need for a dedicated UGC operations employee.

**Legal enabler.** As a VAT-registered intermediary, UGCBoost solves the tax deduction problem that prevents large brands from scaling paid UGC collaborations in Kazakhstan.

## Who This Serves

**Primary — Brand Marketing Teams:** Marketing managers at consumer brands (cosmetics, fashion, FMCG, food, services, events) who want to run mass UGC campaigns but lack the operational capacity. Ranges from multi-brand distributors running parallel campaigns to local businesses testing UGC for the first time.

**Primary — UGC Creators:** Micro-bloggers (typically 1K–50K followers) across Instagram, TikTok, and Threads who create content in categories like fashion, beauty, lifestyle, parenting, and food. They want a steady flow of brand collaborations without publicly advertising themselves as paid creators.

**Operator — Platform Admin:** Aidana (co-founder), who manages creator moderation, quality standards, and campaign operations. Initially manual; moderation decisions accumulate as training data for future AI-assisted screening.

## Marketplace Dynamics

UGCBoost faces the classic chicken-and-egg challenge. The launch strategy addresses this directly:

**Supply-first approach.** Build the creator base first (target: 200 vetted creators), then bring in brands with a ready-to-activate network. Creators are recruited via direct outreach to quality micro-bloggers identified through manual scouting.

**Anchor event.** Eurasian Fashion Week (May 13–14, 2026) serves as the first live test — multiple brand partners, diverse campaign types, real operational validation. Results become the case study for onboarding commercial brands post-event.

**Warm pipeline.** Brands are already waiting. One Corporate, Beauty by Sofia, and First Media Group (Kotex, Ferrero, Milka) reached out organically before the platform existed. Post-Fashion Week, the plan is to approach these and similar brands with a proven case study and a ready-made pool of vetted creators in fashion, beauty, and lifestyle categories.

## Scope

**MVP (target: May 13, 2026) — Telegram Mini App + Admin Panel:**
- Landing page with creator application form (web); on submission, prompt to open Telegram bot for notifications
- Telegram bot for notifications and Mini App entry point; creator onboarding flow: landing → form → Telegram bot → moderation result → contract → platform access
- Creator moderation workflow (LiveDune metrics + manual review)
- Contract signing via TrustMe integration
- Campaign browsing, application, and approval flow
- Work submission (links to published content on Instagram, TikTok, and Threads) with configurable field count per campaign requirements
- Admin panel: creator moderation, campaign management, cross-campaign creator tracking
- Reminder notifications (2 weeks, 1 week, 3 days, 1 day before deadlines)

**Not in MVP:**
- Payment processing (Fashion Week campaigns are barter-only)
- Automated LiveDune analytics dashboard (analytics collected manually or post-event)
- Native mobile app (App Store / Google Play)
- Brand self-service registration portal
- Logistics integration (Relog)
- AI-assisted moderation

## Business Model (Exploratory)

Revenue model is not yet finalized. Three options under consideration:

1. **Commission per transaction** — percentage fee on each paid creator engagement
2. **Tiered subscription** — brands pay monthly for campaign capacity (e.g., Tier 1: 1 campaign/100 creators, Tier 2: 5 campaigns/500 creators)
3. **Hybrid** — monthly subscription for platform access + commission on paid engagements

Additional revenue streams:
- Agency services (contract management, tax intermediary role) for brands requiring legal compliance on UGC spending
- Content licensing fees — brands pay premium for the right to repurpose UGC content as paid ad creatives (Spark Ads, Partnership Ads, targeted ads); UGCBoost controls these rights via creator contracts

Fashion Week MVP operates free of charge to validate the platform before introducing monetization.

## Vision

**Year 1:** Establish UGCBoost as the go-to UGC platform in Kazakhstan. Onboard 20+ active brands, curate 2,000+ vetted creators, prove the model with measurable campaign results.

**Year 2–3:** Expand to adjacent Central Asian markets (Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan) where similar dynamics exist — high social media adoption, booming e-commerce, zero local UGC tooling. Add logistics (integrated delivery via Relog), AI-powered moderation trained on accumulated review data, full analytics automation, creator education program (turning rejected applicants into future supply), and turnkey event UGC coverage packages. Launch native mobile apps.

**Long-term:** Become the regional infrastructure layer for UGC marketing in Central Asia — the platform where every brand runs their UGC seeding and every quality creator finds work, with operational tools that make mass UGC campaigns as easy to launch as running a digital ad.
